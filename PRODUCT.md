# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Jamaican businesses and contractors sourcing haulage services: construction companies, infrastructure project managers, agricultural exporters, and commercial logistics coordinators. They arrive with a specific need (moving aggregates, heavy equipment, or cargo) and want to quickly assess whether ProHaul can handle the job, then make contact. They are decision-makers who value reliability and proven capability over flashy promises.

## Product Purpose

ProHaul's website is a lead-generation landing page for Jamaica's haulage market. It establishes ProHaul as the most credible, capable haulage partner on the island — grounded in a founding legacy — and converts that trust into quote enquiries. Success means a visitor understands the scope of services, believes in the company's track record, and submits a contact form or picks up the phone.

## Brand Personality

Bold, Professional, Modern. A company that has earned its reputation through decades of hard work and is not shy about saying so. Confident without being arrogant. Industrial strength with a clean, considered presentation that signals the company has grown beyond "local operator" into a genuine logistics partner.

## Anti-references

- **Generic logistics SaaS** (Flexport, Convoy, Transfix): Blue/white palette, feature-grid layouts, SaaS-clean typography, hero-metrics bar. ProHaul is a physical, people-first operation — not a platform.
- **Cheap local directory site**: Cluttered layout, low-resolution stock photos, inconsistent type, no visual hierarchy. ProHaul should feel premium and intentional at every touch point.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the work, not the promise** — use real photography, concrete service names, and specific detail over marketing abstraction.
2. **Legacy earns the right to boldness** — the founder's story and 20+ years of operation are proof points; the design should carry that weight without retreating to corporate safety.
3. **Red is the brand, not the decoration** — the red/black/white system is the identity; every use of color should be intentional and load-bearing, never decorative filler.
4. **Mobile-first conviction** — the target market includes site managers and operators accessing on phones; mobile UX is not an afterthought.
5. **Friction-free to enquiry** — every section should make it easier to reach the contact form; CTAs are directional, not decorative.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target: WCAG AA minimum. The audience includes users on lower-end Android devices and slower mobile connections — performance and readability under real-world conditions matter as much as Lighthouse scores. Reduced motion support required.
